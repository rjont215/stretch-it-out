Before you start

Warm up first. Do these right after one of your walks, or move for 3–5 minutes first — cold, hard calf stretching is exactly what tends to pop.

Left hip. These target tight glutes, hip flexors and rotation. If a stretch reproduces your walking pain rather than a normal stretch pull — especially the deep-flexion ones — back off that one. If a few weeks of this doesn't help or it worsens, get it looked at.

Right shoulder. Everything here stays gentle and out of loaded end-range overhead. Keep dead hangs slightly active, and stop short of any pinch.

The clock is a guide, not a rule. Stretch to sensation, never sharp pain.
